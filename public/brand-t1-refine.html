§ My read
Of the five

Variant C (taller ticks) is the strongest. Here's why.

A is the baseline, and the thing that's off about it is that the ticks look shy — they sit inside the frame with generous padding top and bottom, which makes them read as decorative lines rather than a measurement. C fixes that by pushing the ticks almost to the edges. The ticks stop being shy. They become a unit.

B (heavier ticks) is close behind but the 4px ticks make the favicon feel chunky at 16px. The ticks merge into the frame visually.

D (wider gap) is elegant but the wider interval weakens the object — the two ticks start to read as separate marks rather than a paired unit. The mark loses its cohesion.

E (frameless) is the one to pay attention to. I included it as a test and I'm 60/40 against it but it's worth your eyes. The frame is doing real work — it says "this is one unit, contained" — but the frameless version has its own argument: the ticks are the measurement, full stop, no container required. E also reads as more design-y in a way that may or may not be what you want. Worth considering.

My pick is C. Runner-up is E if you want the frame gone.
