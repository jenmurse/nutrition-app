§ TOOLBAR BUTTON OPTIONS · POST-11 · V3

One button treatment. Three toolbars.

The edge-anchored full-height button only works because it borrows the terminal slot's frame logic — it connects to the viewport edge and reads as part of the structure. Pulled out of that slot (mid-toolbar on Planner), it turns into a heavy block with nothing to connect to. That means it's a compositional trick, not a primitive, and building the button system around a trick produces inconsistency.

The cleaner rule: all toolbar CTAs are compact sharp black rectangles, vertically centered, sized to their content. Same across Recipes, Pantry, and Planner. The button's weight comes from its fill, not its height. Position on the row (terminal vs mid) is irrelevant to how the button looks.

The button looks identical to the other two pages. Only its position in the row differs.

The rule this session settled: Toolbar CTAs are compact sharp black rectangles, vertically centered, sized to content. One treatment. The button's weight comes from fill — the only filled shape in an otherwise text-and-hairlines row — not from height or position. This applies across Recipes, Pantry, and Planner. BRIEF-12 will specify this as the definitive button primitive to replace whatever the current implementation has.

Two controls with a 10px gap between them. The field button has a small caret (▾) tucked tight against the label with a 4px gap — reads as one unit, signaling "click here to change what you're sorting by." The direction toggle is a separate button at 11px (slightly bigger than the 9px mono labels) so the arrow glyph feels equal-weight to the labels visually despite being typographically larger. The caret uses top: -1px and font-size: 8px to tame its vertical footprint so it doesn't push the label's line box.

Both the field button's text and the direction button sit on the same baseline as COMPARE, GRID, LIST, and SEARCH in the row above. Toggle the guide at the top of the page to verify visually.
