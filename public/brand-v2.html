§ Brand Exploration · v2

Three refinements, and a final field.

v1 surfaced the right questions but not the right answers. The tick at nav size read as a separator, not a mark. The divided circle was beautiful but under-specified. This round refines both, adds a new option (the paired tick), and keeps Direction 01 (wordmark only) as the control.

First, a design note: the mark doesn't need to say "good." That word is already in the wordmark. The mark's job is to add one thing the wordmark can't — and trying to encode both words produces contrived hybrids. "Good" lives in the tone of the system (the paper, the sage, the voice). The mark carries measure, and only measure.

Direction 05
Wordmark + blocked tick
The tick, with weight

A tick that reads as a shape, not a line.

v1's tick was a hairline. At 14px it disappeared into the type baseline. This version makes the tick a block — 3px wide at nav size, scaling to a real slab at poster. It reads as a deliberate mark rather than an accidental pipe character. The ruler idea survives but the form has enough presence to hold a logo role.

What it's doing
Making the tick a shape with weight. At nav size it reads as a block; at poster size it reads as a ruler's base-line. The mark scales clean in both directions.
What it costs
Risk that at nav size the block looks like a bullet or a bar — unless the proportions (3px wide × 12px tall) are held carefully. Less "signature" than a more complex mark.
Favicon / app icon
The block rotated 90° becomes a horizontal bar, or stays vertical as-is. Works, but a single rectangle is under-specified as an icon.
Cascade
Margin ruler stays. The block becomes a consistent "attention mark" in the app — next to pinned meals, current day, active filters.
Direction 06
Wordmark + interval
The interval — two ticks, one measurement

Measurement is the space between two marks.

Not a single tick — two ticks with a defined gap between them. This is what measurement actually is: not a mark, but the interval between marks. The pair is inherently unique (no common logo uses paired ticks), reads clearly at small size, and the gap is the metaphor. The mark itself measures.

What it's doing
Using the gap as the content. Two ticks define an interval — the interval is the measurement. The mark contains the concept rather than pointing at it.
What it costs
Takes more horizontal space at small sizes than a single tick. Risk of reading as a square-bracket shape ([ ]) or as parentheses without context.
Favicon / app icon
Strong. Two vertical bars in a square, with a measurement-suggesting gap between them. Feels like a unit, which is exactly the brand.
Cascade
The interval becomes a repeating device across the app — delimiting nutrition values, bracketing time ranges, framing daily totals. Very strong system potential.
Direction 07
Wordmark + gauge
The circle, as an instrument

A circle with a reading. Not a division — a measurement.

v1's divided circle was a geometry. This version is an instrument: the horizontal line sits just above center, like a level or a meter at rest. It reads specifically as a measuring device — a gauge, a scale, a level indicator — rather than generically as a circle-with-line. The specificity is in the slight offset.

What it's doing
Turning a symmetric geometry into an instrument. The offset line reads as a reading — a measurement being taken, not a circle being divided.
What it costs
The offset is subtle. If not held precisely (5.5/14 at nav; 19/48 at section), it reads as a centered line again and the specificity collapses. Requires pixel-level discipline in every rendering.
Favicon / app icon
Very strong at favicon size. A circle-with-a-reading inside a rounded square is an iOS-ready icon that is unmistakably unique.
Cascade
The offset line can vary across the app as a live indicator — current nutrition level, progress against goal, a household's hydration. The mark becomes functional.

§ My read
Revised after v1

Direction 06 (paired ticks) is the strongest of this round. It's the only mark where the metaphor is built into the form rather than pointed at by it. A single tick represents measurement. Two ticks with a gap are a measurement. That's a meaningful difference — marks that contain their meaning age better than marks that symbolize it.

It's also the most generative as a system. The interval can expand or contract, bracket a value, frame a range, or sit as a stamp. At favicon size it's two bars and a gap, which is distinct and iOS-ready. In the app it becomes a native device for delimiting values (nutrition ranges, calorie targets, day totals).

Direction 07 (gauge) is the most beautiful, but it's the most fragile. The offset line is the entire idea — if it ever centers, the concept collapses. Requires discipline forever.

Direction 05 (blocked tick) is workable but reads slightly generic at nav size — a single vertical block could be many things. It's safe but not special.

Direction 01 (wordmark only) remains the control. If none of 05/06/07 convince, this is the path of least regret. You lose the signature but keep all of the restraint.
