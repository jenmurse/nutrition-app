§ Mark comparison · Original vs Alt

Two marks. Same concept, different form.

Original: two paired ticks in a square frame. The mark is an interval — the space between two measurements.

Alt: three ticks — two full-height at the edges, one half-height center. Reads simultaneously as an M letterform, a ruler with a midpoint marker, and a measurement interval. More content in the mark; more to read.

§ My read
Honest take

The alt mark is doing more, and that's both its strength and its risk. Three ticks where two existed means the mark is visually denser. At display size it's compelling — the M-shape reads clearly and the ruler-midpoint interpretation adds a layer. The mark has more to say.

At 18px nav and 16px favicon, the center tick becomes a problem. It's half-height and roughly the same width as the outer ticks. At small scale the three elements merge into a visual muddle — it stops reading as "M" and starts reading as noise. The original's gap (the interval concept) was legible at 16px because there was real empty space between two clean shapes. The alt loses that clarity as it shrinks.

Deciding factor: where does the mark live most? If it's primarily a display/poster/header mark and the favicon can be a simplified version, the alt is worth pursuing. If it needs to work at nav size first, the original is safer. That's the question only you can answer — it depends on how you intend to use the mark in the app and on physical goods.
