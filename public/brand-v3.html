Balance. The mark holds the wordmark, not stands beside it.

You said 06 is conceptually right but felt off. The issue isn't the geometry of the paired ticks — it's that they were sitting to the left of the wordmark, unbalanced. Here are three resolutions that put the mark and wordmark into a symmetrical relationship.

The "with good measure" framing (from the URL) reinforces this direction: the product is a companion, and the mark should feel like something the wordmark is held within, not preceded by.

Fix A
Bracketed wordmark
The wordmark as the measurement

One tick on each side. The name is what's being measured.

The paired ticks bracket the wordmark instead of preceding it. Good Measure sits inside the interval — the name is the thing being measured. Symmetrical, balanced, and conceptually clean: you're the measurement. The brackets are the act.

Fix B
Caliper — tight brackets
A measuring instrument holding the name

Same structure, tighter gap. The ticks close in on the name.

The same bracket geometry as Fix A, but the ticks sit closer to the wordmark — like a caliper set to exactly the object being measured. More intimate, more precise. The name fits snugly within its marks. Reads as an instrument that has taken a reading.

Fix C
Display lockup · stacked
Primary lockup + nav lockup (two variants)

The mark lives above the wordmark at display size. Disappears at nav.

Two ticks centered above "Good Measure," used at display sizes only. At nav size, the mark drops and the wordmark stands alone. This gives you a moment (hero, app open, poster) where the full identity shows, and a rest state (nav, favicon, inline) where it simplifies. Two lockups, one identity.

§ My read
Where I'd put my money

Fix A (bracketed, wider gap) is my pick.

The wider gap gives the wordmark room to breathe. The mark reads as a careful bracket — like the name is being set onto a shelf, or placed between two posts, or measured precisely. It's the most balanced composition and the one that most clearly holds the wordmark rather than clutching it.

Fix B (tighter caliper) is conceptually sharper but visually cramped — at nav size the right tick almost touches the final "e," which could read as a typo. Fix C (stacked) is gorgeous at display and effectively Direction 01 at nav — you're choosing to lose the mark 99% of the time in exchange for an occasional display moment.

Fix A is the one that earns the mark in every context. The bracket is quiet enough to disappear when you don't need it, present enough to register when you do, and it balances — which is the word you used, and which matches the app's actual job: keeping things in balance, quietly, reliably.
