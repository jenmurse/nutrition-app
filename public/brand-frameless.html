§ My read

At 48px and 96px the frameless mark is clearly the better object. The ticks at full em height sit as typographic equals beside the wordmark — same weight, same stature. It reads as a ligature or a diacritic, something that belongs to the name rather than something placed beside it.

At 14px nav the frameless mark is very small — two tiny verticals. The gap between them is readable but the whole mark is unassuming to the point of almost disappearing. That might actually be right — the nav doesn't need a loud mark, it needs a quiet signature. But it's worth seeing on your actual screen before deciding.

For the favicon: use option B — dark background with white ticks cut out. The dark square gives the icon a defined boundary (which it needs at 16px to read as a distinct tab icon), and the white ticks maintain the mark's identity. It's the only favicon approach that's both legible and honest to the frameless concept.
