Your questions

Q · Are the MAY / JUN tags visible?

Yes — but in this new draft they're rendered as a small vertical divider between months with the abbreviation floating above it, instead of sitting on top of a single cell. Cleaner than my first pass.

Q · Does the bar scroll horizontally?

Yes. On desktop it fits without scroll on wide screens (~1100px+ of usable width) but scrolls if the planner is narrower. On mobile it always scrolls. The viewport snaps softly to keep the current week roughly centered on initial load, but users can drag left or right to look at past or future weeks.

Q · How does it work on mobile?

Cells shrink to ~28px wide and the strip always scrolls. It lives between the date/arrow toolbar and the 7-day day-strip (the one with Sun/Mon/Tue tap targets that already exists). The day-strip stays primary — the 30-day strip is just for sensing past/future. Tap a day → matrix jumps to that week and the day-strip updates to show that week's days.

What clicking does

Click any day in the strip → the planner navigates to the plan covering that week. If no plan exists yet for that week, we could either (a) show an empty matrix with "Create plan for this week" or (b) make those days dim/unclickable. I'd start with (b) to keep it pure-navigation, no surprise plan creation.
