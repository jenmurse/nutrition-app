§ Alt mark · geometry tuning

Five variants. One question: which survives at 16px?

The alt mark's three ticks merge at small scale because gaps ≈ tick width. The fix is wider gaps, thinner center tick, or both. Five variants below — read the favicon row first.

The variable being tuned: gap-to-tick ratio. At original the ratio is ~0.9:1 (gap slightly narrower than tick). Target is at least 1.5:1 for the gaps to read as space at 16px.

§ My read

C is the likely answer. Wider outer stance (x=8.0/21.26) gives the gaps real room. Thinner center tick (1.6 vs 2.74) makes the midpoint marker read as a different kind of element — a reference notch, not a third full tick. The M-shape reads clearly at display, the ruler-midpoint idea survives to section size, and the favicon lands as two dominant ticks with a small marker between them.

D (hairline center) is cleaner at favicon — the 1.0px center tick may disappear at 16px, which is actually fine since the favicon would then just be two bars (readable), not three elements of varying weight. Trade-off: at display size a 1-unit hairline looks deliberately delicate, which either reads as refined or as fragile depending on your view.

B fails — wider stance without thinning the center just spreads the same problem. Three ticks of equal width at any gap still read as noise when small.

E is overcorrected — the thinner outer ticks undercut the match to the wordmark stem weight. The mark starts to look feathery beside "Good Measure" at 600.

Pick C or D. Then take the geometry into Illustrator, fine-tune the proportions by eye, and send the SVG back. I'll apply it everywhere.
