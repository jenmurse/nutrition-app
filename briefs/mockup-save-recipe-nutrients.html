Save Recipe — full nutrient panel

The card currently shows only 5 macros (cal, protein, fiber, sodium, sugar). But you can optimize for any of the 9 tracked nutrients — sat fat, added sugar, carbs, total fat. And when you optimize one, you want to confirm you didn't wreck another. Three ways to show the full picture.

§ Option 1 — Full vertical panel (all 9 nutrients)
Good Measure
Brown Butter Banana Bread GF Cookies (Lower Sat Fat) — butter out, almond butter + grapeseed oil in, chocolate trimmed. Sat fat drops from 8g to 2g per cookie. Everything else holds steady.

All 9 nutrients, vertical, matching the planner's daily-totals language.
• Changed rows show before → after, color-coded (sat fat ↓ green, protein ↑ green)
• Unchanged rows dim to grey with just the single value — they recede so the
  changes pop
• The optimized nutrient (Saturated Fat) gets a coral left-rule so the eye lands
  on what you asked for
Tradeoff: tallest option (~9 rows). But this is a deliberate save — reviewing
full nutrition impact is the whole point.

then by magnitude of change). Unchanged nutrients fold into one muted line.
Shorter card, changes front-and-center. Tradeoff: you can't see the absolute
