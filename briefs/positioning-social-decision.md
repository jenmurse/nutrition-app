# Positioning + social decision record

Closes the open positioning question in `monetization-decision.md` §10. The tier
decision (two-tier, AI/MCP gated to Pro) forced a positioning choice, because the
product's two wow moments now sit on **opposite sides of the paywall**. This doc
picks the lane and lays out the social plan that follows. The landing-page
rewrite is downstream of this and still pending.

---

## 1. The decision in one paragraph

Good Measure **leads with AI** — specifically the moment of an AI agent acting
*inside the real app* (rewrite a recipe by talking to your own Claude, watch the
nutrition update to the gram). This is the one thing no funded competitor can
show and the only face-free, coverage-worthy social asset in reach. To keep that
hook honest for a brand-new Free user, a **free Pro trial** ships at native
launch so every install actually experiences the AI before the gate. The **day
optimizer** is the strong secondary beat: "and it also does this on its own, no
AI needed" — which doubles as reassurance that the Free product stays useful
after the trial.

---

## 2. The constraint that shapes everything

**No on-camera. No personal brand.** (User preference + `launch-viability-plan.md`
§8.) This rules out the entire genre the category lives in — founder-to-camera
reels, cooking process videos, UGC reaction clips. The whole category's social
playbook (Peel, SomeYum, the TikTok/Instagram recipe-import apps) is closed to us
by constraint. Everything here is **screenshot / screen-recording only.** That is
a limitation, but it forces us onto the one lane where we're actually
differentiated.

---

## 3. The two wow moments (and which side of the paywall each sits on)

| | **Day optimizer** | **Recipe AI editing** |
|---|---|---|
| The moment | Off-target day → tap Optimize → 3 options → totals snap green | Type one sentence to Claude → it reads the real recipe → swaps to the gram → nutrition panel updates |
| Tier | **Free** (capped) | **Pro / MCP** (needs their own Claude) |
| True for a cold viewer? | Yes — anyone can do it free, day one | Only after they're Pro + connected |
| Differentiation | "App does a smart thing" — adjacent to category claims | **Un-copyable** — no competitor's AI acts *inside* the app |
| Press value | Low (useful, not novel) | **High** — "I connected Claude to my meal planner" is a real story |

Key fact from the competitive read (`launch-viability-plan.md` §8): rival AI
planners return suggestions *in a chat you then act on yourself*. Good Measure's
AI **acts** — it writes the change back. The category's own reviewers handed over
the framing: rival tools "can write a meal plan as text but cannot act on it."
The entire AI-lane content strategy is proving that sentence false, on screen,
repeatedly.

---

## 4. Why lead with AI (not the safe optimizer-first lane)

The market wants AI, and — more importantly — **the AI clip is the only asset
that competes against nothing.** A web search of the category's live social
confirms it: everyone else's distribution is video-import and recipe-discovery
swiping, all of which needs a face or a kitchen on camera. The optimizer clip is
genuinely nice (the editorial design makes "numbers turn green" photograph well)
but it competes in the crowded "an app does a smart thing" space, and meal
planning is useful-not-viral, so it converts the already-frustrated searcher
without spreading.

The AI-acting-inside-the-app clip is:
- **Face-free** (it's a screen recording of an interface).
- **Un-copyable** (no funded competitor can show their AI writing back into the
  real app).
- **Coverage-worthy** to the exact channel the viability doc names — AI-tool
  newsletters and tech-curious writers, who don't need a founder's face, just a
  sharp writeup. The MCP architecture, a liability for normal users, is the
  *interesting* thing here.

So the social plan argues **for** the lane Jen was already leaning toward. Lead
with AI.

---

## 5. The catch, and the fix (why the free trial is load-bearing)

Leading with AI creates one honesty problem: the AI is Pro + requires the
viewer's own Claude. A stranger who loves the clip, installs Free, gets none of
it — the front-door problem resurfacing as a marketing problem.

**The fix is the free Pro trial** (14-day, card up front, MCP included — see
`monetization-decision.md` §10 and the native-launch mechanics discussed). It is
what lets the best social clip and the landing page tell the **same true story**:
every new install really does get the AI at first. The trial is not optional
polish here; it is the thing that makes the lead-with-AI strategy honest. It also
doubles as the conversion mechanic (let them feel the whole thing, then gate).

The trial is a **native-launch** feature (store-managed via Apple/Google).
Friends-and-family are `comp` (everything free), so no trial is needed now. This
is captured so the page rewrite and the trial ship together, not out of order.

---

## 6. The three-beat narrative (page + social share one spine)

1. **Hook — AI acts inside the app.** The recipe-rewrite-via-Claude moment. One
   sentence in, gram-level swaps written back, nutrition updates. This is the
   attention beat and the press beat.
2. **Credibility — precision.** The exact-number swap (miso 4 tsp → 2 tsp,
   low-sodium soy, 1,858 → 950 mg sodium). The thing a nutrition-literate viewer
   knows other apps fake. This is what separates "cute AI demo" from "this is
   real."
3. **Reassurance — it stands on its own.** The day optimizer doing its
   three-options thing with **no AI at all**, plus the household angle. This tells
   the trial-ended user the Free product is genuinely useful, softening the gate,
   and tells the no-Claude user there's a real tool here for them too.

Same spine for the landing page and the social account. Write once, deploy as
page section + clip + carousel.

---

## 7. Content units (all screenshot / screen-recording, no face)

Carried from `launch-viability-plan.md` §8, now sequenced under the AI-lead:

- **"One sentence, one rewrite" clip** (primary, the hook). Recipe AI editing via
  Claude — type the goal, watch the gram-level swaps land and the panel update.
  15–30s. Remade per goal (lower-sodium, higher-protein, swap-for-what's-in-the-
  pantry). Each maps onto a Playbook story.
- **"Off-target day, fixed by the numbers" clip** (the optimizer, secondary
  beat). Unbalanced day in red → Optimize → 3 options → apply → green. The
  "it works without AI too" proof. Free, reachable, reassuring.
- **Before/after recipe card** (carousel). Nutrition panel before/after
  optimization with the exact swaps. Beautiful given the editorial design, pure
  screenshots. Pinterest/IG format.
- **The household angle** (the followable story, not a brochure). Jen + Garth, one
  kitchen, two goals, shared pantry. "One dinner that hits two different macro
  targets." Relatable, real, not personal-brand-y. Almost no app can tell it.

**The Playbook is the marketing engine, not a help doc.** Each story is written
once and deployed as tutorial + carousel + clip. (See `playbook-stories.md`.)

## 8. Distribution / pitching (the actual risk)

Product quality is not the bottleneck — **distribution is** (MealThinker: a
near-twin with one rating). The AI lane is chosen partly *because* it's the one
that earns coverage without a face.

- **Targets:** AI-tool newsletters ("I connected Claude to my meal planner and it
  rewrote my week" is novel to a tech-curious reader) and individual food /
  nutrition newsletter writers with a "deliberate cook" audience.
- **NOT** the big "best nutrition app 2026" review sites — those are pay-to-play
  SEO mills (the §1 Tier-3 players game them).
- **What to hand them:** a no-friction account on the **free Pro trial** (NOT an
  MCP install task) plus a "try these three sentences" card so they hit the wow in
  the first 90 seconds. Top failure mode: signup → no fast wow → silent churn.
  **Engineer the first 90 seconds.** (This is also why the slow-load fix is a
  pre-pitch blocker — `launch-viability-plan.md` §0.5.)
- **Honest expectation:** this converts the already-frustrated AI-meal-planning
  searcher; it probably does not go viral. A warm, high-intent, small audience —
  which suits the no-personal-brand temperament.

**Sequence:** landing page rewritten → Playbook stories → social built from the
stories.
